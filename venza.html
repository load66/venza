🔧 Oil Change — ValvoMax
Every 5,000 mi (custom) · ~30 min · $34.54 · Toyota spec: 10k/12 mo
▾
⚠ OIL ONLY: SAE 0W-16 Full Synthetic — Mobil 1 5 qt from Walmart. The 0W-20 label looks nearly identical. Verify before pouring. Toyota manual confirms: 0W-20 only acceptable as one-time emergency substitute.
Parts: Mobil 1 0W-16 (5 qt) · Filter 90915-YZZN1 · ValvoMax drain bag kit · Fresh gloves + rags
1
Confirm oil label — verify 0W-16 in bold on jug. Double-check before opening.
2
Warm engine 5–10 min, then shut off and cool 10–15 min. Oil should be warm (flows better) but not scalding hot.
3
Drive onto ramps, apply parking brake firmly, place wheel chocks behind rear tires.
4
Crack oil filler cap on top of engine — do not remove, just loosen to allow air flow during drain.
5
Remove undercover panel — 4 screws, 10mm socket. Snug only when reinstalling. Never overtighten plastic fasteners.
6
Attach ValvoMax drain bag — screw drain tube onto valve clockwise. Oil flows automatically. Let drain 15 full minutes for complete drain.
7
Swap filter while draining — Smart Jerry wrench, HEX side only to break loose (never use the square drive to loosen — can fracture). Hand-thread new filter then tighten to 13 ft-lbs (18 Nm) or hand-snug + ¾ turn.
8
Close drain valve — unscrew drain tube counterclockwise. Oil stops instantly. Reinstall the steel safety cap hand-tight.
9
Reinstall undercover — 4 screws, 10mm, finger-snug only. Do not torque plastic.
10
Lower vehicle, add 4.5 qt of 0W-16. Start engine, idle 60–90 sec, shut off. Wait 5 min. Check dipstick — top off to upper hole. Total target: 4.8 qt.
11
Visual leak check — inspect under car around drain valve and filter. Should be completely dry.
12
Reset oil life — Settings → Vehicle → Maintenance Reset → Oil Maintenance Required.
13
Dispose free at AutoZone or O'Reilly in Webb City. Sealed container — they pump it out and accept used filters too.
💡 Dipstick (yellow handle): Upper hole = FULL. Lower hole = ADD. Always wipe, reinsert fully, pull again — first pull gives false high reading. Slightly under full is safer than overfilled.
🌡️ Engine Coolant Drain & Fill
Due ~100,000 mi / 120 months · Car Care Nut method
▾
⚠ FLUID ONLY: Toyota Super Long Life Coolant (SLLC) Part# 00272-SLLC2 — pink, pre-mixed 50/50. DO NOT use universal green/orange coolants. Toyota manual specifies SLLC or "similar non-silicate, non-amine, non-borate ethylene-glycol with long-life hybrid organic acid technology."
Important: Venza Hybrid has TWO separate cooling systems — Engine coolant (100k first replacement) and Inverter coolant (150k first replacement). Both use Toyota SLLC pink. Best reference videos: search "2019+ RAV4 Hybrid coolant change" — same A25A-FXS engine.
Tools needed: Lisle 24680 spill-free funnel · 5/16" ID drain hose · Drain pan · Toyota SLLC coolant
1
Let engine cool completely — minimum 2 hours after driving. Opening hot coolant system is dangerous.
2
Locate radiator petcock at bottom of radiator. Attach a 5/16" ID hose to direct coolant into drain pan. Open petcock — drain fully.
3
No water flush needed — Toyota manual and Car Care Nut method: straight drain and refill is correct. Flushing with water is unnecessary on a properly maintained cooling system.
4
Close petcock once fully drained.
5
Attach Lisle 24680 spill-free funnel to radiator fill neck. This prevents air pockets and messy spills.
6
Fill with Toyota SLLC (pre-mixed 50/50) through the funnel until the funnel stays consistently full. Leave funnel attached.
7
Activate Maintenance Mode — required to run gas engine for air bleeding. Sequence: Gas pedal 2x → Neutral → 2x → Park → 2x → Start. Engine will run in gas mode.
8
Hold ~2,000 RPM until the cooling fan cycles twice. Continue adding coolant to funnel as the level drops — air is purging from the system.
9
Remove funnel, cap off. Let engine cool completely. Check coolant reservoir — top off to FULL line with Toyota SLLC if needed.
💡 Inverter coolant service is separate at ~150k miles. The inverter cooling system has its own reservoir (check your owner's manual for location). Both reservoirs take the same Toyota SLLC pink coolant.
💨 Cabin Air Filter
~15,000 mi (custom) · Toyota spec: 30k · ~5 min DIY
▾
Part: A-Premium Activated Carbon APCA4605 — $11.18. Activated carbon removes odors in addition to dust and pollen. Toyota manual notes: replace sooner if airflow from A/C is reduced or windows fog in Fresh mode.
1
Open glove box and completely empty its contents.
2
Squeeze sides of glove box inward — this compresses the side pins past the stops. Lower the glove box door fully.
3
Remove filter housing cover — unclip or slide the rectangular cover behind the open glove box opening.
4
Slide out old filter. Note the airflow direction arrow on the filter frame — the new one must match.
5
Insert new filter with arrow pointing in the same direction (typically toward the cabin/blower). Seat flat with no gaps at the edges.
6
Replace cover, restore glove box. Squeeze sides again to click back into stops. Done — no tools required.
💡 Toyota manual footnote: "Driving in heavy traffic, on dirt roads or in urban, dusty or desert areas may shorten filter life." If you notice reduced A/C airflow or foggy windows in Fresh mode, replace sooner regardless of mileage.
🌬️ Engine Air Filter
~30,000 mi per Toyota manual · Part# 17801-YZZ16
▾
Part: Toyota OEM #17801-YZZ16 — $15.59 at Frank Fletcher Toyota Parts. Toyota manual says: inspect for damage, excessive wear, and oiliness — replace if necessary at 30k intervals.
1
Open hood. Locate the air filter box — black rectangular plastic housing toward the front/side of the engine bay.
2
Release the clips securing the air box lid — typically spring clips on each side. Open the lid.
3
Remove old filter. Inspect the housing interior and wipe out any debris with a dry cloth.
4
Insert new filter — ensure all 4 edges seat flat against the housing with no gaps that could let unfiltered air bypass.
5
Close lid, secure clips. Done — no tools required for most clip-style housings.
💡 Quick inspection method: hold the old filter up to a light source. If you can't easily see light through it, it's due for replacement regardless of mileage interval.
🔋 HV Battery Cooling Filter
Inspect every 5,000 mi · Clean every 20,000 mi · Per Toyota manual
▾
Toyota Manual says: "Visually check the HV battery cooling intake filter for dirt or dust every 5,000 miles and clean if necessary. Clean every 20,000 miles." This is critical for hybrid battery longevity.
1
Locate the HV battery cooling intake — it is typically behind the rear seat area or in the cargo compartment area. Refer to your owner's manual for exact location on your specific Venza.
2
Every 5,000 mi — Inspect: Visually check for visible dust buildup, lint, or debris. If lightly dusty, a gentle tap or light vacuum is sufficient.
3
Every 20,000 mi — Clean: Remove the filter cover, take out the filter element, and clean with low-pressure air or a soft brush. Do not use water or wet cleaning.
4
Reinstall filter and cover. Ensure no gap that could allow unfiltered air into the battery cooling system.
💡 A clogged HV battery cooling filter can cause the hybrid battery to run hotter, which reduces battery life over time. This is one of the few hybrid-specific maintenance items that matters long-term.
🔄 Tire Rotation
Every 5,000 mi · Lug nut torque: 80 ft-lbs
▾
AWD Pattern — Forward Cross: Rear tires move straight to the front. Front tires cross to opposite rear (RF → LR, LF → RR).
1
Loosen all 5 lug nuts per wheel while tires are still on the ground — break them loose only, don't remove yet.
2
Jack up and support one corner at a time. Place jack stand under pinch weld point. Never work under a car on just a jack.
3
Swap per forward cross pattern. While wheels are off, visually inspect brake pads for wear and rotors for scoring or rust.
4
Hand-thread lug nuts in a star/cross pattern (never circular). Lower vehicle until tire contacts ground but doesn't fully carry weight.
5
Torque to 80 ft-lbs in star pattern with torque wrench. Lower fully, then do a final torque check on each lug.
6
Check tire pressures — target 33 PSI all four corners. Top up if needed.
💡 Current tread depth as of 02/24/26: LF/RF/LR at 6/32", RR at 7/32". Toyota recommends replacement at 2/32". Plan for new tires together with alignment to maximize value.
🔩 ValvoMax — First-Time Install
One-time only · Confirm torque at valvomax.com first
▾
⚠ CRITICAL: ValvoMax official torque spec for M12-1.25 is NOT confirmed from manufacturer documentation. Third-party reference: EZ Oil Drain Valve lists 20 ft-lbs for M12-1.25. Verify directly at valvomax.com before proceeding.
What you need: ValvoMax M12-1.25 · 24mm socket · 1/2" drive torque wrench · Thread-clean rag · Fresh crush washer (included)
1
Do this during your first oil change — do steps 1–6 of oil change guide first (drain old oil through OEM plug before installing ValvoMax).
2
Remove OEM drain plug — 14mm socket, 30 ft-lbs. Let oil drain completely. Save crush washer only if ValvoMax can't be installed today.
3
Clean drain hole threads — wipe the oil pan's drain port threads with a clean dry rag. Remove any old gasket material or grit that could prevent O-ring from seating.
4
Install crush washer on ValvoMax — this is a one-time use washer (not needed on future oil changes).
5
Thread ValvoMax by hand clockwise until snug. Then torque to manufacturer-specified value (verify at valvomax.com first) using 24mm socket. Do NOT overtorque — can distort O-ring seat and cause weep.
6
Continue oil change — add oil, start engine, run 2 minutes, shut off. Do a visual inspection under the car — drain valve area should be completely dry.
7
Valve is now permanent — never remove it. All future oil drains: attach drain bag tube → oil flows. Unscrew tube → oil stops instantly.
💡 No crush washer needed on any future oil change — the VITON O-ring triple seal is the permanent seal. This saves ~$0.50 per change and one less step.
⚙️ Oil Life Reset
After every oil change · 30 seconds
▾
Toyota manual: "Reset the oil replacement reminder light ('MAINT REQD') or the message 'OIL MAINTENANCE REQUIRED' on the multi-information display after maintenance at every 5,000 miles."
1
Turn vehicle to ON/Ready mode (power on, don't start engine).
2
Go to: Settings → Vehicle → Maintenance Reset → Oil Maintenance Required
3
Confirm the reset. "MAINT REQD" light should extinguish.
💡 If the message doesn't appear, you may need to scroll through the multi-information display using the steering wheel controls until you find the maintenance reset menu.
