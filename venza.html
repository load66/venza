🛢️ Oil Change — ValvoMax Installed
Every 5,000 mi · ~30 min · $34.54 · Mobil 1 0W-16 + filter 90915-YZZN1
▾
⚠ 0W-16 FULL SYNTHETIC ONLY: The hybrid engine uses a computer-controlled variable-speed oil pump calibrated for 0W-16. Using 0W-20 can interfere with pump calibration. Check the jug label every time — 0W-16 and 0W-20 look nearly identical on the shelf. Emergency one-time use of 0W-20 is acceptable; replace with 0W-16 at the very next change.
Supplies: Mobil 1 0W-16 Full Synthetic 5 qt (~$27.97 Walmart) · OEM Filter 90915-YZZN1 (~$6.57 Toyota Parts) · ValvoMax Starter Kit drain bag · Nitrile gloves + rags
Torque Reference: Oil filter: 13 ft-lbs / 18 Nm (or hand-snug + ¾ turn) · Access panel screws: snug only — do not overtighten plastic · ValvoMax valve: already permanent, never re-torque
1
Verify oil label before opening — confirm 0W-16 in bold. Do not proceed with wrong viscosity.
2
Warm engine 5–10 min, then cool 10–15 min — warm oil drains faster and carries more contaminants out. Cool enough to work safely.
3
Drive onto ramps on flat concrete — front wheels fully seated on VEVOR ramps. Apply parking brake firmly. Place wheel chocks behind both rear tires.
4
Crack oil filler cap counterclockwise — just enough to break vacuum seal. Leave cap resting in place. This allows oil to drain without air lock.
5
Remove underbody access panel — 4 screws, 10mm socket. Keep screws in your pocket so they don't roll away.
6
Attach ValvoMax drain bag and open valve — thread drain tube counterclockwise onto valve until it seats. Oil begins flowing automatically. Walk away for 15 full minutes. No tools, no mess — unscrew tube to stop flow instantly.
7
Swap oil filter while draining — Smart Jerry 64mm wrench, HEX side only to break loose (never the square 3/8″ drive hole — can fracture housing). Apply thin film of fresh 0W-16 to new filter O-ring. Hand-thread new filter until O-ring contacts housing, then ¾ turn more only. Torque spec: 13 ft-lbs / 18 Nm. Never install with wrench.
8
Close ValvoMax valve — once oil flow stops (~15 min), unscrew drain tube counterclockwise. Oil stops the instant tube disengages. Thread steel safety cap back on finger-tight.
9
Reinstall underbody panel — snap panel back up. 4 screws, 10mm, snug only. Do not overtighten — plastic panel bosses can crack.
10
Lower vehicle, fill with 4.5 qt of 0W-16 — drive off ramps carefully. Use funnel, pour into filler neck. Reinstall filler cap.
11
Check dipstick and top off — wipe yellow dipstick clean, reinsert fully until seated, pull again. Add oil in ¼-qt increments to reach upper hole. Total target: 4.8 qt. Never overfill — overfill causes foaming and engine damage.
12
Start engine, idle 60–90 sec, inspect for leaks — oil pressure light may briefly stay on while passages fill (normal). Shut off, inspect under car. Completely dry around valve and filter. If any drip from ValvoMax: tighten ¼ turn at a time, max 18 ft-lbs total.
13
Final dipstick check after 5 min — wait 5 minutes after shutdown, then verify oil level one final time at upper hole.
14
Reset oil life monitor — Settings → Vehicle → Maintenance Reset → Oil Maintenance Required. Write next due mileage (current + 5,000) on ValvoMax sticker.
15
Dispose of used oil free — AutoZone 1024 S Madison, Webb City (M–Sa 7:30AM–8PM, Su 8AM–8PM) or O'Reilly 1001 S Madison, Webb City (M–Sa 7:30AM–9PM). Bring in any sealed container. Used filters also accepted.
💡 Dipstick reference: Upper hole = FULL. Lower hole = ADD 1 qt. Below lower = critical, do not drive. Above upper = overfilled, drain some out. Always check on level ground — wipe, reinsert fully, pull again. First pull right after filling gives false high reading.
🔩 ValvoMax — First-Time Install
One-time only · Do during first oil change · Valve stays permanent forever
▾
⚠ ONE-TIME PROCEDURE: Do this ONCE during your first oil change. After the ValvoMax is installed, skip to the regular Oil Change guide above for every future service. The valve stays permanent — never remove it.
What you need: ValvoMax M12-1.25 Quick Twist valve · ValvoMax Starter Kit drain bag · 14mm socket (OEM plug removal, this time only) · 24mm socket · 1/2″ drive torque wrench (10–150 ft-lb) · 10mm socket (access panel) · Clean rag
Torque Reference: OEM drain plug (removed this time only): 30 ft-lbs · ValvoMax M12-1.25 (one time only, permanent): 16–18 ft-lbs · Oil filter: 13 ft-lbs / 18 Nm or hand + ¾ turn · Access panel screws: snug only
1
Warm engine 5–10 min, then cool 10–15 min — warm oil drains faster and carries more contaminants out. Do not drain scalding oil. Let cool until safe to touch.
2
Drive onto ramps on flat, level concrete — VEVOR ramps on flat surface. Drive slowly until front wheels are fully seated on top. Set parking brake. Chocks behind both rear wheels. Never work under a vehicle supported only by a floor jack.
3
Crack oil filler cap counterclockwise — just enough to break vacuum seal. Leave resting in filler neck. Allows oil to drain freely without air lock.
4
Remove underbody access panel — 10mm socket, 4 screws. Set screws somewhere safe.
5
Attach ValvoMax drain bag, then remove OEM drain plug — connect Starter Kit drain bag per ValvoMax instructions. Use 14mm socket to remove OEM drain plug counterclockwise. Torque reference (for info only): OEM reinstall would be 30 ft-lbs. Let oil drain fully — 15+ minutes. Keep the OEM drain plug as a spare.
6
Clean drain port threads thoroughly — use clean, dry rag to wipe out the drain opening. Remove any old gasket material or grit. Threads must be clean and dry for the ValvoMax O-rings to seat correctly.
7
Hand-thread ValvoMax M12-1.25 straight into drain port — thread in straight by hand, do NOT cross-thread. Rotate the valve body 360° to choose the drain port orientation for convenient future access. Position chosen now is permanent.
8
Torque to exactly 16–18 ft-lbs using 24mm socket and 1/2″ drive torque wrench. Do NOT exceed 18 ft-lbs — overtightening can damage the O-ring sealing surface. This is the only time you will ever torque this valve. It stays here permanently.
9
Swap oil filter — Smart Jerry 64mm wrench, HEX side only to break loose, then hand-unscrew. Apply thin film of fresh 0W-16 to new filter O-ring. Hand-thread until O-ring contacts housing, then ¾ turn more. Torque spec: 13 ft-lbs / 18 Nm. Never use wrench to install.
10
Reinstall access panel — snap panel back up. 10mm, 4 screws, snug only.
11
Lower vehicle, fill with 4.5 qt of 0W-16 — drive off ramps. Use funnel into filler neck. Reinstall filler cap finger-tight.
12
Dipstick check — top off to upper hole — wipe, reinsert fully, pull again. Add in ¼-qt increments. Total target: 4.8 qt. Slightly under full is better than overfilled.
13
Start engine, idle 60–90 sec, inspect for leaks — oil pressure light may briefly stay on (normal, passages filling). Shut off after 90 sec. Inspect under car: ValvoMax and filter must be completely dry. If any drip from ValvoMax: tighten ¼ turn at a time, max 18 ft-lbs total.
14
Final dipstick check after 5 min, reset oil monitor — wait 5 min after shutdown, verify at upper hole. Reset: Settings → Vehicle → Maintenance Reset → Oil. Write next service mileage on ValvoMax sticker.
💡 No crush washer needed on any future oil change — the ValvoMax VITON O-ring triple seal is the permanent seal. All future drains: thread on drain tube → oil flows → unscrew tube → oil stops instantly. No tools, no mess, no crush washer ever again.
🌡️ Engine Coolant Drain & Fill
Due ~100,000 mi / 120 months first change · Toyota SLLC pink pre-mixed 50/50
▾
⚠ FLUID ONLY: Toyota Super Long Life Coolant (SLLC) Part# 00272-SLLC2 — pink, pre-mixed 50/50. DO NOT use universal green/orange coolants. Toyota specifies non-silicate, non-amine, non-borate ethylene-glycol with long-life hybrid organic acid technology (HOAT).
Important — Two Separate Systems: The Venza Hybrid has TWO independent cooling loops: Engine coolant (100k first change) and Inverter coolant (150k first change). Both use Toyota SLLC pink. Best reference videos: 2019+ RAV4 Hybrid coolant change — same A25A-FXS engine.
Parts & Tools: Toyota SLLC #00272-SLLC2 (pre-mixed 50/50) · Lisle 24680 spill-free funnel · 5/16″ ID drain hose · Drain pan · Petcock is at radiator bottom — no torque spec, hand-snug only when closing
1
Let engine cool completely — minimum 2 hours after driving. Opening a hot pressurized cooling system causes serious burns.
2
Locate radiator petcock at the bottom of the radiator. Attach a 5/16″ ID hose to the petcock spout to direct coolant into drain pan. Open petcock — drain fully.
3
No water flush needed — Toyota manual and Car Care Nut method: straight drain-and-refill is correct procedure. Flushing with water dilutes the new coolant unnecessarily on a well-maintained system.
4
Close petcock finger-snug once fully drained. Do not overtighten — plastic petcock threads are easy to strip.
5
Attach Lisle 24680 spill-free funnel to radiator fill neck. This funnel prevents air pockets and messy overfill spills during bleeding.
6
Fill with Toyota SLLC through the funnel until funnel stays consistently full. Leave funnel attached during the bleeding step.
7
Activate Maintenance Mode to run gas engine for air bleeding. Sequence: Gas pedal 2× → Neutral → 2× → Park → 2× → Start. Engine will run in gas mode with coolant circulating.
8
Hold ~2,000 RPM until cooling fan cycles twice — add coolant to funnel as level drops. Air is purging from the system. Fan cycling twice confirms the thermostat has opened and the system is fully bled.
9
Remove funnel, install radiator cap. Let engine cool completely, then check coolant reservoir — top off to FULL line with Toyota SLLC if needed.
💡 Inverter coolant service is separate at ~150k miles. The inverter cooling loop has its own reservoir and bleeding procedure (consult owner's manual for reservoir location). Both systems use identical Toyota SLLC pink coolant.
💨 Cabin Air Filter
~15,000 mi (custom) · Toyota spec: 30,000 mi · ~5 min · No tools
▾
Part: A-Premium Activated Carbon APCA4605 (Item #1004KW2G) — $11.18 at a-premium.com. Activated carbon removes odors in addition to dust and pollen. No torque specs — no tools required for this job.
1
Open glove box and empty completely — remove all contents before proceeding.
2
Squeeze sides of glove box inward — compresses the side pins past the stops, allowing the box to lower fully past the normal open position.
3
Remove filter housing cover — unclip or slide the rectangular cover behind the open glove box opening. Set aside.
4
Note the airflow direction arrow on the old filter before removing. The new filter must match this orientation exactly.
5
Slide out old filter, insert new filter with arrow pointing in the same direction (typically toward cabin/blower). Seat flat with no gaps — unfiltered air bypassing the edges reduces filtration effectiveness.
6
Replace housing cover, restore glove box — squeeze sides to click back into stops. Done. No tools required.
💡 Toyota manual: "Replace sooner if airflow from A/C is reduced or windows fog in Fresh mode." Also replace sooner if driving frequently in heavy traffic, on dirt roads, or in dusty areas.
🌬️ Engine Air Filter
Toyota spec: 30,000 mi · Part# 17801-YZZ16 · ~5 min · No torque specs
▾
Part: Toyota OEM #17801-YZZ16 (105A Element) — $17.11 at Frank Fletcher Toyota Parts. Toyota manual: inspect for damage, excessive wear, and oiliness at 30k intervals. No torque specs — spring-clip housing, no tools required.
1
Open hood. Locate the air filter box — black rectangular plastic housing toward the front/side of the engine bay.
2
Release spring clips on each side of the air box lid — press outward to release. Open lid fully.
3
Remove old filter. Use a dry cloth to wipe out any debris from the housing interior before installing the new one.
4
Insert new filter — all 4 edges must seat flat against the housing with no gaps. Any gap allows unfiltered air into the engine, bypassing the filter entirely.
5
Close lid, secure all clips. Confirm each clip is fully seated. Done.
💡 Quick inspection: hold old filter up to a bright light. If you cannot easily see light through it, replace regardless of mileage. Also replace if you see oil contamination on the filter — that indicates an engine issue worth investigating.
⚡ Spark Plugs
Toyota interval: 120,000 mi · Qty 4 · OEM: 90919-01289 · Torque: 18 ft-lbs
▾
⚠ COLD ENGINE ONLY + HYBRID SAFETY: Do this on a fully cooled engine — aluminum cylinder head can be damaged by hot plug removal. Keep key away from engine bay — hybrid system can wake up and energize HV components. Do NOT touch orange wiring or connectors at any time.
OEM plug: Toyota 90919-01289 (replaces 90919-A1001) · Denso FC16HR-Q8 equivalent · Qty: 4 plugs
Torque Reference: Spark plug: 18 ft-lbs / 25 N·m · Ignition coil bolt: 80 in-lbs / 9 N·m (10mm) · Tools: 14mm thin-wall spark plug socket · 10mm socket · 3/8″ ratchet · 6″ extension · torque wrench
1
Cool engine fully, remove engine cover — open hood, remove plastic engine cover (spring clips or bolts depending on trim). Place key far away from engine bay.
2
Locate 4 ignition coils on top of the inline-4 engine. Each coil sits directly over one spark plug well.
3
Work one cylinder at a time — unplug the coil connector (press lock tab), remove single 10mm coil bolt, torque spec: 80 in-lbs / 9 N·m. Twist and pull coil straight up. Check coil boot for oil, cracking, or carbon tracking.
4
Clean plug well before opening — blow out the spark plug tube with compressed air or vacuum it out. Critical step — keeps grit out of the cylinder.
5
Remove old plug with 14mm thin-wall spark plug socket + 6″ extension. Break loose gently (cold aluminum), then spin out by hand.
6
Verify new plug matches — Toyota 90919-01289 / Denso FC16HR-Q8. Hand-start new plug until it spins in freely. If resistance is felt immediately, stop and restart — cross-threading aluminum heads is a serious repair.
7
Torque new plug to 18 ft-lbs / 25 N·m with 14mm plug socket and torque wrench. Do not overtighten — aluminum head threads are easily damaged.
8
Reinstall ignition coil — reseat coil in well, torque bolt to 80 in-lbs / 9 N·m, reconnect electrical plug until it clicks. Repeat steps 3–8 for remaining 3 cylinders.
9
Reinstall engine cover, start vehicle — confirm smooth idle, no warning lights. If misfire appears immediately, shut off and recheck coil connectors.
💡 Replace all 4 as a set, one cylinder at a time. Never leave a plug out while moving to the next — keeping them in sequence prevents mix-ups. Log the service immediately so the app resets your next spark plug interval.
🔋 HV Battery Cooling Filter
Inspect every 5,000 mi · Clean every 20,000 mi · Per Toyota manual · Near rear seat
▾
Toyota Manual: "Visually check the HV battery cooling intake filter for dirt or dust every 5,000 miles and clean if necessary. Clean every 20,000 miles." No torque specs — no fasteners on filter itself. Cover clips are hand-pressure only.
1
Locate HV battery cooling intake — near the rear seat area (consult owner's manual for exact location on your Venza trim). The filter protects the HV battery from dust intake.
2
Every 5,000 mi — Inspect: Visually check for visible dust buildup, lint, or debris clogging the mesh. If lightly dusty, a gentle tap or light vacuum from outside is sufficient — no disassembly needed.
3
Every 20,000 mi — Full clean: Remove cover (press-fit clips, hand pressure only). Take out filter element. Clean with low-pressure compressed air from inside-out, or soft brush. Do NOT use water or any wet cleaning method.
4
Reinstall filter and cover — ensure no gaps. Unfiltered air entering the battery cooling path accelerates battery degradation over time.
💡 This is the most commonly neglected hybrid-specific maintenance item. A clogged filter causes the HV battery to run hotter, directly shortening battery pack life. A pack replacement on a 2021 Venza is a multi-thousand dollar repair.
🔄 Tire Rotation
Every 5,000 mi · Lug nut torque: 80 ft-lbs · AWD forward-cross pattern
▾
AWD Pattern — Forward Cross: Rear tires move straight to the front same side. Front tires cross to opposite rear (RF → LR, LF → RR). This is the correct pattern for AWD vehicles per Toyota manual.
Torque Reference: Lug nuts: 80 ft-lbs / 108 N·m · Thread: M12-1.5 · Socket: 21mm · Torque in star/cross pattern (never circular) · Target pressure: 33 PSI all four corners (cold)
1
Loosen all lug nuts with vehicle on ground — break loose only, do not remove. Breaking loose while the tire is on the ground prevents hub rotation. Use 21mm socket.
2
Lift and support one corner at a time — place jack stand under pinch weld (see owner's manual for correct jack point). Never work under a vehicle supported only by a floor jack.
3
Swap per forward-cross pattern — while wheels are off, inspect brake pads for wear indicator grooves and rotors for deep scoring, heat cracks, or heavy rust.
4
Hand-thread lug nuts in star pattern — never thread in circular order. Lower vehicle until tire contacts ground but doesn't carry full weight.
5
Torque lug nuts to 80 ft-lbs in star pattern — lower fully, then do a final torque pass on all 5 lug nuts per wheel. Do not use impact gun for final torque.
6
Check and set tire pressure — target 33 PSI cold all four corners. Check TPMS light after driving to confirm sensors reset.
💡 Current tread depth as of 02/24/26: LF/RF/LR at 6/32″, RR at 7/32″. Toyota recommends replacement at 2/32″. Plan new tires with a simultaneous alignment to maximize tire life from the start.
⚙️ Oil Life Reset
After every oil change · 30 seconds · In-vehicle menu
▾
Toyota manual: "Reset the oil replacement reminder ('MAINT REQD') after maintenance at every 5,000 miles." The reset only affects the dash reminder — it does not communicate with the engine or affect any systems.
1
Turn vehicle to ON/Ready mode — power on without starting engine (press Power button without brake pedal).
2
Navigate to: Settings → Vehicle → Maintenance Reset → Oil Maintenance Required
3
Confirm the reset. The MAINT REQD light on the instrument cluster should extinguish immediately.
💡 If the maintenance reset menu is not visible, use the steering wheel controls to scroll through the multi-information display until you find it. On some Venza configurations this is accessed through the touchscreen center stack rather than the steering wheel controls.
