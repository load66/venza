🔧 DIY Oil Change (ValvoMax)
Every 5,000 mi · ~30 min · $34.54
▾
⚠ OIL: SAE 0W-16 FULL SYNTHETIC ONLY — Mobil 1 5qt from Walmart. Do NOT use 0W-20 or any other grade.
Parts needed: Mobil 1 0W-16 (5qt) · Filter 90915-YZZN1 · ValvoMax drain bag kit
1
Verify oil bottle label — confirm 0W-16. The 0W-20 label looks nearly identical. Don't trust memory.
2
Warm engine 5–10 min, then cool 10–15 min. Oil should be warm (flows better) but not scalding.
3
Drive onto VEVOR ramps, apply parking brake, place wheel chocks.
4
Loosen oil filler cap on top of engine — don't remove yet, just crack it to allow airflow.
5
Remove access panel — 4 screws, 10mm socket. Snug only when reinstalling, do not overtighten plastic.
6
Attach ValvoMax drain bag — screw the drain tube onto the valve. Oil flows automatically. Wait 15 minutes to drain fully.
7
Swap filter while draining — use Smart Jerry wrench HEX side only to break loose. Hand-thread new filter, then tighten 13 ft-lbs or hand-snug + ¾ turn.
8
Close drain valve — unscrew drain tube. Oil stops instantly. Reinstall steel safety cap.
9
Reinstall access panel — 4 screws, 10mm, snug only.
10
Lower vehicle, add 4.5 qt of 0W-16. Start engine, idle 60–90 sec, shut off. Wait 5 min. Check dipstick — top off to upper hole. Target: 4.8 qt total.
11
Reset oil life: Settings → Vehicle → Maintenance Reset → Oil.
12
Dispose of oil free at AutoZone or O'Reilly in Webb City (sealed container).
💡 Dipstick: Yellow handle. Upper hole = FULL. Lower hole = ADD. Wipe, reinsert fully, pull again for accurate reading. Slightly under full is better than overfilled.
🌡️ Coolant Drain & Fill
Due ~100,000 mi · Car Care Nut method
▾
⚠ FLUID: Toyota Super Long Life Coolant (SLLC) ONLY — Part# 00272-SLLC2, pink, pre-mixed 50/50. Do NOT use universal coolants.
Tools needed: Lisle 24680 spill-free funnel · 5/16" ID drain hose · Drain pan
1
Let engine cool completely — at least 2 hours after driving. Never open coolant system on a hot engine.
2
Locate radiator petcock at the bottom of the radiator. Attach 5/16" ID hose to direct coolant into drain pan.
3
Open petcock and let system drain fully. Do not flush with water — straight drain and refill is the correct method.
4
Close petcock when fully drained.
5
Attach Lisle 24680 spill-free funnel to the radiator fill neck.
6
Fill with Toyota SLLC pink coolant (pre-mixed 50/50) until the funnel stays full. Leave funnel attached.
7
Activate Maintenance Mode — gas pedal 2x → Neutral → 2x → Park → 2x → Start. This runs the gas engine for air bleeding.
8
Hold engine at ~2,000 RPM until cooling fan cycles twice. Add coolant to funnel as level drops during bleeding.
9
Remove funnel, cap system, let engine cool. Check coolant reservoir level — top off to FULL line if needed.
💡 Note: The Venza Hybrid has TWO independent cooling systems (engine + inverter), both using Toyota SLLC pink coolant. Best reference videos: search "2019 RAV4 Hybrid coolant" — same A25A-FXS engine.
💨 Cabin Air Filter
Every ~15,000 mi · ~5 min DIY · A-Premium APCA4605
▾
Part: A-Premium Activated Carbon APCA4605 — $11.18 at a-premium.com
1
Open glove box, empty contents.
2
Squeeze sides of glove box inward to release the stop pins, then lower it fully.
3
Remove the filter cover — slide or unclip the housing cover behind the glove box.
4
Slide out old filter, note airflow direction arrow on frame.
5
Insert new filter with arrow pointing in same direction (toward cabin/blower, usually down or toward rear).
6
Replace cover, reinstall glove box. Done.
💡 Takes about 5 minutes. No tools required. The activated carbon version removes odors in addition to dust/pollen.
🌬️ Engine Air Filter
Every ~30,000 mi · ~5 min DIY · Part# 17801-YZZ16
▾
Part: Toyota OEM #17801-YZZ16 — $15.59 at Frank Fletcher Toyota Parts
1
Open hood. Locate the air filter box — black rectangular plastic box near the front of the engine bay.
2
Release the clips or screws securing the air box lid — typically 4 spring clips or screws on the top.
3
Lift lid, remove old filter. Inspect housing for debris and wipe clean if needed.
4
Insert new filter — ensure it seats flat with no gaps around the edges.
5
Replace lid, secure clips. Done — no tools needed.
💡 Inspect the filter at every oil change by holding it up to light. If it's grey/brown and you can't see light through it easily, replace it.
🔄 Tire Rotation
Every 5,000 mi · Lug nut torque: 80 ft-lbs
▾
Pattern for AWD: Forward cross — rears move straight forward, fronts cross to opposite rear.
1
Loosen all 5 lug nuts per wheel while tires are still on the ground — break them loose but don't remove.
2
Jack up one corner using the designated jack point. Place jack stand. Repeat for each corner being swapped.
3
Remove and swap tires per the forward cross pattern.
4
Hand-thread lug nuts in star pattern. Lower vehicle until tire touches ground but doesn't carry full weight.
5
Torque to 80 ft-lbs in star pattern with torque wrench. Lower fully, confirm torque again.
💡 Always torque in a star/cross pattern — never circular — to ensure even seating of the wheel against the hub.
